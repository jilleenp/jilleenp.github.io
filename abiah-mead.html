Phillips Family Farm

Abiah Mead

Last updated on July 1, 2022

Abiah Mead may have

  • been born Abiah Potter
  • married first Simon Fraiser and had five children, including a daughter Margery Fraiser who later married [[ Hinkley, Nathan|Nathan Hinkley ]]
  • married second [[ Mead, James|James Mead ]], and had no more children

Evidence

  • The FamilySearch family tree suggests that Nathan Hinkley (LYZB-6X8) married Margery Fraser (LYZB-6F9). Margery was the daughter of Simon Fraser (GZ8Y-Z34) and Abiah Potter (MV6T-M1T). (No other children of Simon and Abiah suggested.)
  • This is consistent with information offered in James Mead’s Revolutionary War pension file:
    • Abiah Mead stated that She well recollects from other facts When married to the Said James She was a widow + mother of Five Children the youngest a Daughter and Two Years old the 29. day of June as She was Married in November after. And the Said Child now if living is or was Fifty three years old the 29. day of June last.1
    • David Fraser and William Fraser both offered testimony of Abiah and James. David stated that he had been at the marriage ceremony of the two. David and William may have been brothers of Simon Fraser.
    • David Fraser stated in an affidavit that after Abiah Mead had been widowed, She went to live with her Daughter Mrs. Hinkly.2

  • This would also explain why Nathan Hinkley migrated from Roxbury, Delaware county, to Shandaken, Ulster county, not far from James and Abiah Mead. It also explains why he was able to provide an affidavit stating that he remembered when James and Abiah had been married, and that he had been sent by his father with a horse and wagon to fetch the new bride and groom. It makes sense that he remembered this event fifty years later because he later married the bride’s daughter, and that Abiah lived with Nathan and “Mrs. Hinkley” after she had been widowed.3

  • “The first marriage [in the town of Stamford] was between Simon Frazier and Abiah Potter, Jan, 1787.”4
  • Simon Frasier was listed as a “fence viewer and damage ‘priser” in Stamford, Delaware county, New York, in 1793.5
  • Guardianship for the “children of Simon Fraser, late of Delaware Co.” was granted to James Grant of Stamford on 10 Mar 1809. The children were listed as Donald, age 18 yrs (born about 1788), William, age 16 yrs (born about 1793), Mary, age 15 yrs (born about 1794), and Margaret, age 13 yrs (born about 1796).6
    • This is consistent with Abiah’s claim that her youngest daughter, Margaret, had just turned two when she married James Mead in 1798 or 1799.
