Nettie Mead's Letter to the Editor

Last updated on July 16, 2022

Where the Research Started

In July of 1919, Nettie Belle Mead Edwards wrote a letter to the editor of the Putnum County Courier:1

WOULD LIKE DATA ON NORTH SALEM RELATIVES The following letter has been received at this office and it is hoped some of our readers may be of assistance to Mrs. Edwards in tracing her relatives: Christopher, Ill., July 28, 1919. City Editor, North Salem, N.Y.

Dear Sir:

I am trying to find if any of my ancestors fought in the Revolutionary War, and that perhaps you could assist me.

My great grandfather, Nathaniel Mead, was born in North Salem, Mar. 21, 1777.

He was married to Lucy Ganung who was born in Southeast, Putnum Co., N.Y., Oct. 29, 1779. They were married Nov. 16, 1797, but have not been able to find out at what place.

My grandfather, Leonard Wesley Mead, was born in Sourtheast, Sept. 26, 1806. He was married to Ann Hammond, but do not know when or where she was born.

I want to find out names of Nathaniel’s father and mother, when and where born, and when and where married.

It seems there would be records of some kind that would give me information I want.

If no other perhaps church records, or those found on tombstones.

If you cannot help me out, perhaps you can tell me someone who can.

Thanking you in advance, I am Very truly yours, (Mrs. A. U.) NETTIE MEAD EDWARDS

My father was Alfred Mead, born in Roxbury, N.Y.

I should also like to know more of Ganungs and Hammonds.

Census records show that Alfred Mead resided in the household of Leonard and Ann Mead, along with his brother, Henry H. Mead.

The specificity of the dates and locations Nettie provides in the note suggests that she may have been referencing a family Bible, as opposed to oral family history. Nettie applied for a delayed certificate of birth with the state of Iowa in 1957. She stated that she was born on 15 Jun 1873, near Moulton, Appanoose county, Iowa. Her parents were Alfred Theodore Mead (born in Roxbury, New York) and Sarah Armstrong (born Port Byron, Illinois). Nettie, who was living in Beckley, West Virginia, signed the application, stating for the veracity of the statements. Either the notary public or state register who also signed the document indicated that Nettie’s supporting document included a family bible record, which also gave her birth as 15 Jun 1873. It is possible that the dates and locations she cited in her letter came from this same family Bible.2
