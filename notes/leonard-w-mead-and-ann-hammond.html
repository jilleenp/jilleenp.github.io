Leonard W. Mead and Ann Hammond

Last updated on July 30, 2022

Leonard W. Mead1 (b. 14 Sep 1805, in Putnam County, New York; m. Ann Hammond, about 1830; d. 3 Mar 1888, in Prattsville, Greene County, New York)
Leonard Mead’s parents were Nathaniel Mead and Lucy Ganoung
Family Search ID: KH4M-3GV

Ann Hammond (b. about 1811, possibly in Delaware County, New York; m. Leonard Wesley Mead, about 1830; d. between 1865 and 1870, in Delaware County, New York)
Ann Hammond’s parents have not been identified (see Research Notes for Ann Hammond)
Family Search ID: MCFW-78F

Leonard and Ann had the following children:
1. Mary Paulina “Polly” (b. Nov 1833; m. John L. Price; d. 1913, probably in Delaware County, New York)
2. Alfred Theodore (b. 1835; m. Sarah Armstrong; d. 22 Sep 1913, in Christopher, Franklin County, Illinois)2
3. John F. (b. 8 Mar 1838; m. (1) Caroline A.; m. (2) Hannah Holiday, 22 Feb 18653; d. 11 Apr 1886, in Halcott, Delaware County, New York)
4. James Manette (b. 14 Nov 1839, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; m. Phila Jane King, about 1865; d. 25 May 1901; served in Civil War)

6. Mary Elizabeth (b. May 1843; m. John Henry Myers, abt 1861; d. after 1930) 7. Garner (b. 1845; d. probably between 1850 and 1855)

11. Hiram (b. Oct 1853; m. Edith A. ?, 1877; d. after 1925)

They also had three additional children who apparently died too early to have been named in a census record.4

Life Sketch

Leonard W. Mead was born 14 Sep 1805 in Putnam County, New York.5 His parents were Nathaniel Mead and Lucy Ganoung.6 Ann Hammond was born to unknown parents likely between 31 May 1810 and 1 Jun 1812, possibly in Delaware County, New York.7

Leonard and Ann may have married 23 Mar 1831, almost certainly in Delaware County.8

On 25 Mar 1837, Leonard and Ann were living in Roxbury when they took a mortgae for $225 against their fifty-five acre farm.9 In 1839, they were threatend with foreclosure, and in 1843 they transfered the farm to Leonard’s brother, Alfred H. Mead, for only one dollar.10

Ann probably died between 1865 and 1870, as she was last identified in the 1865 census. By 1875, Leonard had moved to Conesville, Schoharie county, New York, to live with his daughter, Mary Elizabeth, and her husband John Meyers. In 1880, John and Mary Elizabeth had moved their family to Prattsville in Greene county, and Leonard was living with his son, Nathaniel, in Conesville.

Leonard W. Mead died in Prattsville on 2 March 1888 at 1:40 in the morning. His cause of death was recorded as “paralysis and old age.”6
