Abiah Potter

Last updated on July 24, 2022

Abiah Potter (b. abt 1774; m. (1) Simon Fraiser, Jan 1887; m. (2) James Mead, about 1798; d. after 1850)

The five children of Simon Frasier and Abiah Potter included:1

  • Possibly Daniel (born about 1787)
  • Donald (born about 1788)
  • William (born about 1793)
  • Mary (born about 1794)
  • Margaret (born about 1796)

Abiah did not have any children with James Mead.

FamilySearch ID: MV6T-M1T

Life Sketch

Abiah Potter probably first married Simon Fraiser:

“The first marriage [in the town of Stamford] was between Simon Frazier and Abiah Potter, Jan, 1787.”2

Simon Fraiser was listed listed as a “fence viewer and damage ‘priser” in Stamford, Delaware county, New York, in 1793.3 On 10 Mar 1809, guardianship for the “children of Simon Fraser, late of Delaware Co.” was granted to James Grant of Stamford. The children were listed as Donald, age 18 yrs (born about 1788), William, age 16 yrs (born about 1793), Mary, age 15 yrs (born about 1794), and Margaret, age 13 yrs (born about 1796).4

After Simon’s death, Abiah remarried James Mead in 1798 or 1799. In James Mead’s Revolutionary War pension file, Abiah stated that

She well recollects from other facts When married to the Said James She was a widow + mother of Five Children the youngest a Daughter and Two Years old the 29. day of June as She was Married in November after. And the Said Child now if living is or was Fifty three years old the 29. day of June last.5

Daniel Fraser and William Fraser, who may have been sons of Simon Fraser and Abiah Potter, both testified to the marriage of Abiah and James. Daniel stated that he had been at the marriage ceremony of the two. Daniel also stated in an affidavit that after Abiah Mead had been widowed by James Mead’s death

She went to live with her Daughter Mrs. Hinkly.6

Abiah’s daughter “Mrs. Hinkly” was probably Margery or Margaret, who likely married Nathan Hinkley. Nathan provided an affidavit that he remembered when James and Abiah had been married, and that he had been sent by his father with a horse and wagon to fetch the new bride and groom.7 It makes sense that he remembered this event fifty years later if he later married the bride’s daughter, and that Abiah lived with Nathan and “Mrs. Hinkley” after she had been widowed.

In 1830, James Mead was enumerated in Shandaken, Ulster county, New York, not far from Daniel Frazer and William Frazer. Nathan Hinkley was listed in the 1840 census living in Shandaken next door to the households of Daniel Fraser and William Fraser. Nathan Hinkley likely migrated from Roxbury, Delaware county, New York, to Shandaken to remain near his wife’s family.

Abiah probably had no children with James Mead. Abiah died sometime after 1850 when she last provided testimony for James’ widow’s pension.
