Jonathan Hammond and Polly Jenkins

Last updated on August 5, 2022

Jonathan Hammond (b. probably between 1780 and 17841; m. possibly (1) Mary Hinckley in Patterson, Putnam County, New York2; m. Polly Jenkins3; d. unknown)
Jonathan’s parents are unknown.
Family Search ID: L679-86P

Polly Jenkins (b. about 1785; m. Jonathan Hammond; d. unknown)
Polly Jenkins’ parents were Nathaniel Jenkins and Anna Elizabeth “Betsy” Robinson.
Family Search ID: KNWZ-GFY

Jonathan’s known children were:
1. Delight “Litta” (b. about 1806, in Delaware County, New York; m. Joseph Embree Scudder, about 1830; d. 1877, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; b. Moresville Burying Ground)
2. Mary Ann “Polly” (b. about 1815; m. Daniel D. Keator, about 1840; d. about 1860, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York)
3. Nathaniel (b. 15 Oct 1815; m. Caroline Sears, about 1837; d. 1856, in Shandaken, Ulster County, New York)
4. Phoebe (b. 18 Feb 1818, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; m. Alfred Whitney, about 1835; d. 12 Jun 1893, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; b. Halcott Cemetery)
5. Margaret Hammond (b. 26 May 1819, in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; m. Alfred Hartwell Mead, 12 Jun 1827; d. 20 Oct 1900, in Yates Center, Kansas)
6. James Monroe (b. 12 Sept 1822; m. Hannah Robinson, about 1827; d. 10 Apr 1897 in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York; b. Halcottsville Cemetery)

Life Sketch

Jonathan Hammond was probably born between 1780 and 1784. A sketch written by his grandson, Richard S. Hammond, suggests that he “came to Roxbury from Long Island and settled on a small farm and built a log house.”2 No further evidence of Jonathan coming from Long Island has been found.

According to that same sketch, Jonathan Hammond married Polly Jenkins, and they had at least six known children. Jonathan was living in Roxbury, Delaware County, New York, by 1810, with his wife and four daughters, only one of whom has been positively identified (Litta, born about 1806).

In October of 1824, Jonathan purchased just under two acres of land in Roxbury from John and Phoebe Vermeilya. The property was described as being on the westerly bank of the Batavia Kill stream, bordering “the north line of the farm of Jonathan Hammond.”4 Three years later, he purchased for $350 an adjoining lot of sixty acres, and an additional thirty acres for another $350.5 The property abutted the farm of Nathaniel Robinson, Ebenzer Ganoung, and John C. Keator.

Jonathan and Polly took a mortgage of $470 against their approximately ninety-two acres of land from Nathaniel Robinson in September of 1832.6

It was said that “the first tannery was built and operated by Jonathan Hammond about [1807], on the place now owned by James Hammond.”7 James M. Hammond continued to live in his father’s farm until his death in 1897.

Jonathan and Polly were last identified in Roxbury in the 1840 census. It is not known when or where they died.
