Remember to document family friends!

Last updated on July 6, 2022

It is important for the family historian to document today’s key family events. it is perhaps even more important to document those relationships that no records will ever reveal to tomorrow’s researchers.

My son has an Uncle Ron, and Aunt Tammy and a cousin Brendan. No legal records exist that would ever explain to a researcher who these people are and why they figure into so many of our family stories.

And then there’s Aunt Esta. I have a picture, labeled in an old hand, of “Fred, Joe and the Aunts.” Later someone added a bit a clarifying text that it was “Aunt Alice, Aunt Esta and Aunt Ida.”

I knew Alice and Ida to be sisters of my great-grandmother, but no where in my research had I found any mention of an Esta. And she was clearly known by people who had personally told me family stories.

It wasn’t until I happened upon a letter that “Aunt Esta” had written to a great-aunt when my grandfather passed away that I had enough to clues to piece together that she was…Aunt Alice’s “motoring” buddy. Sigh.

Couldn’t someone have just written that down for me?
